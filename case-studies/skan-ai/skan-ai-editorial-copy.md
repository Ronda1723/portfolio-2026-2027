## SECTION 1 — THE PROBLEM

Skan AI builds agentic process intelligence for the Fortune 500. Their core technology is a Large Process Model — a multimodal vision-language system that understands enterprise workflows by watching how humans do them, then deploys AI agents to do the same work with built-in self-correction and KPI-driven optimization.

The technology is deep. The audience for the pitch — investors, enterprise buyers, partners — needs to understand it in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes.

The pitch deck wasn't doing that. The slides looked like engineering schematics: dense, hierarchical, accurate, and impossible to parse at a glance. The story Skan needed to tell was buried under the technical complexity that made the story worth telling in the first place.

That's the problem I was brought in to solve.

## SECTION 2 — THE WORK

I designed across two engagements: a comprehensive pitch deck redesign with the founder, then a full rebuild of an investor deck used in a real pitch to Insight Partners.

The work covered every layer of a deck: visual hierarchy, custom iconography, layered depth, chart design, slide-level rebuilds, and a coherent visual language across 50+ slides. I designed the customer maturity model — a five-stage horizontal flow showing how Skan customers progress from Process Excellence through Agentic AI. I rebuilt the "Challenge for Agentic AI" slide three times until it held the narrative weight it needed. I redesigned the ACV growth chart with proportional scaling that preserved the historical story.

The hardest deliverable was a series of dense Draw.io architecture diagrams that needed to become editable, professional PowerPoint slides. The diagrams covered Skan's full agentic architecture — data ingestion, screen comprehension, process state recognition, agent execution loops, LLM-as-judge evaluation, KPI-driven optimization, exception handling. Forty-nine technical concepts in one diagram alone.

I converted them into widescreen, fully editable slides where every box could be moved, every label could be retyped, every connector remained intact. The technical accuracy stayed. The legibility was the deliverable.

For the Insight Partners deck specifically, I took an existing 19-slide presentation plus a folder of raw enhancement materials and produced a polished 41-slide investor presentation in 48 hours.

## SECTION 3 — FIVE SKILLS THIS WORK REQUIRED

**1. Translation of complexity into clarity.**
Taking technical AI/ML concepts — multimodal VLMs, agentic execution loops, LLM-as-judge evaluation systems — and making them legible to investors without dumbing them down or losing accuracy.

**2. Visual storytelling at the slide-narrative level.**
Every slide has a job. Designing each one to do its specific narrative work in the deck — progression, authority, system, scale — instead of just looking polished.

**3. Iconography, layout, and depth.**
Building a custom visual language across 50+ slides — consistent stack metaphors, custom icons for technical concepts, layered backgrounds that suggest architecture without explaining it.

**4. Editability discipline.**
Every slide delivered as fully editable elements. No flattened images, no grouped objects, no shortcuts that prevent live iteration. Founders need to fix typos and adjust layouts up until the moment they walk into the room.

**5. Speed and reliability.**
Forty-one investor-ready slides in 48 hours. Slide rebuilds in under an hour. Speed isn't the opposite of quality — at this scale of work, the two are inseparable.
